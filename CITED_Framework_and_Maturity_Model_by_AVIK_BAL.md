# The CITED Framework

**A growth operating system for visibility in the age of AI search.**

> Classic SEO optimized for ten blue links. AI search rewards something different: being read, trusted, and cited inside the answer itself. CITED is the framework for earning that citation. Five pillars, one mechanism, a maturity path you can place yourself on today.

---

## Why CITED exists

Search changed faster than the playbooks did.

A rising share of queries now end inside an AI-generated answer: Google's AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini. In that world, ranking is not the finish line. The new question is whether an AI engine reads your content, trusts it, and cites it when it writes the answer.

Getting ranked and getting cited are different games. Most frameworks only teach the first.

CITED is built for the second. It is a structured system, not a checklist of tactics, organized around five pillars that together determine whether your brand shows up when an AI answers a buyer's question. Each pillar reinforces the others, and together they create a compounding effect: the Citation Flywheel.

---

## The five pillars

CITED stands for **C**itation Surface, **I**nformation Architecture, **T**echnical Foundation, **E**vidence-Rich Extraction, and **D**emand Capture. Read top to bottom, they move from "can an engine see you" to "does that visibility convert."

### C — Citation Surface

**The question it answers:** Across how many places, in how many forms, does your brand appear where AI engines look?

Citation Surface is your total footprint in the sources AI engines draw from. A single great page is a small surface. A brand mentioned across your own site, third-party publications, comparison sites, communities, reviews, and structured data has a large one. AI engines favor entities that appear consistently across many trusted sources, because corroboration is how they decide what to trust.

You expand Citation Surface by being present and consistent wherever your category is discussed, not just on your own domain.

### I — Information Architecture

**The question it answers:** Is your content organized so an engine can find the exact passage that answers a given question?

AI engines do not cite whole pages. They lift passages. Information Architecture is how cleanly your content is structured into retrievable, self-contained units: clear headings, logical topic clusters, internal linking that signals relationships, and passages that make sense when pulled out of the page. Strong architecture turns a wall of text into a library an engine can navigate.

This is where topical authority is built: a well-architected cluster of related content signals genuine depth on a subject.

### T — Technical Foundation

**The question it answers:** Can AI crawlers actually access, render, and parse your content?

The unglamorous prerequisite. If AI bots cannot reach your content, nothing else matters. Technical Foundation covers crawler access (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended not blocked), clean server-rendered HTML rather than content trapped in unrendered JavaScript, fast load times, structured data that classifies your content, and signals like `llms.txt` that guide AI crawlers. It is the plumbing that lets the other four pillars function.

### E — Evidence-Rich Extraction

**The question it answers:** Once retrieved, is your content specific and credible enough to be chosen and quoted?

Retrieval gets you into the candidate pool. Extraction is about being the passage the engine actually quotes. AI engines favor content that is concrete: specific numbers, named sources, direct answers stated up front, verifiable claims, and clear definitions. Vague, hedged, generic content rarely gets cited because an engine cannot safely lift it. Evidence-Rich Extraction means writing so that every key claim is quotable, attributable, and hard to argue with.

This is the pillar most brands underinvest in, and the one that most separates cited brands from invisible ones.

### D — Demand Capture

**The question it answers:** When an AI engine cites you, does that visibility turn into pipeline?

Visibility without conversion is vanity. Demand Capture is the bridge from citation to business outcome: ensuring the pages AI engines cite also move a reader toward action, that branded search lift from AI mentions is captured, and that your funnel is ready to receive the demand that AI visibility creates. A citation that sends a curious buyer to a dead-end page is a wasted win.

## The Citation Flywheel

The pillars are not a one-time checklist. They compound.

A strong **Technical Foundation** lets engines access content. Good **Information Architecture** makes that content retrievable. **Evidence-Rich Extraction** makes it citable. Each citation expands your **Citation Surface**, which increases the corroboration signals that make future citations more likely. And **Demand Capture** turns that growing visibility into revenue, which funds more content and more surface.

```
   Citation Surface ──► more corroboration ──► more citations
          ▲                                          │
          │                                          ▼
   Demand Capture ◄── Evidence-Rich ◄── Information ◄── Technical
     (revenue)         Extraction        Architecture    Foundation
          │                                          ▲
          └──────────► funds more content ───────────┘
```

The more you are cited, the more citable you become. That is the flywheel, and it is why CITED is an operating system rather than a campaign.

## The CITED Maturity Model

A framework is only useful if you can place yourself on it. The Maturity Model defines five stages of AI search readiness. Find where you are, then work the next pillar gap to move up.

### Level 1 — Invisible

AI engines cannot see you, or have no reason to cite you.

- AI crawlers may be blocked or content is trapped in unrendered JavaScript
- Content is thin, generic, or absent on the questions buyers ask
- No structured data, no consistent presence beyond the homepage
- You do not appear in AI answers for your own category

**The move up:** fix the Technical Foundation first. You cannot be cited if you cannot be crawled.

### Level 2 — Crawlable

Engines can access you, but rarely choose you.

- AI bots can reach and render your content
- Some useful pages exist, but they are unstructured and hard to extract from
- Occasional, accidental citations, but no consistency
- Competitors are cited far more often for shared queries

**The move up:** invest in Information Architecture. Organize content into clear, retrievable passages and topic clusters.

### Level 3 — Retrievable

Engines find and pull your content, but you are one option among many.

- Content is well-structured and engines retrieve it for relevant queries
- You are cited sometimes, but inconsistently and rarely as the primary source
- Claims are present but not always specific or well-evidenced
- Citation Surface is limited mostly to your own domain

**The move up:** strengthen Evidence-Rich Extraction. Make your passages the most specific, credible, quotable answer available.

### Level 4 — Cited

You are a regular, trusted source in AI answers for your category.

- AI engines cite you consistently for your core topics
- Your content is specific, evidence-rich, and structured for extraction
- Your Citation Surface spans your domain plus third-party sources
- Citations are starting to influence branded search and pipeline

**The move up:** close the loop with Demand Capture. Ensure every cited page converts the visibility into measurable business outcomes.

### Level 5 — Compounding

The flywheel is turning. Citations drive demand, demand funds growth, growth expands surface.

- You are a default cited authority across your category's questions
- Citation Surface is broad and self-reinforcing through corroboration
- AI visibility is a measurable, attributed source of pipeline
- New content earns citations faster because your entity is already trusted

**At this level the work shifts** from earning citations to defending and extending a compounding advantage competitors struggle to catch.

## Self-Scorecard

Rate your organization on each pillar from 1 to 5. Be honest; the gaps are where the growth is.

### Citation Surface

| Score | You are here if... |
|---|---|
| 1 | Present only on your own homepage; no third-party mentions |
| 2 | Some owned content, minimal external presence |
| 3 | Solid owned content across key topics; limited third-party citations |
| 4 | Consistent presence across owned and several trusted external sources |
| 5 | Broad, corroborated presence wherever your category is discussed |

### Information Architecture

| Score | You are here if... |
|---|---|
| 1 | Flat, disorganized content; no clear structure |
| 2 | Some headings and pages, but no topic clustering or internal linking |
| 3 | Clear page structure; emerging topic clusters |
| 4 | Strong clusters, internal linking, and retrievable passages throughout |
| 5 | Architecture signals deep topical authority; every passage is self-contained and extractable |

### Technical Foundation

| Score | You are here if... |
|---|---|
| 1 | AI crawlers blocked or content not rendering |
| 2 | Crawlable but slow, with little structured data |
| 3 | Accessible, reasonably fast, some schema in place |
| 4 | Clean rendering, fast, structured data on key pages, AI crawlers welcomed |
| 5 | Fully optimized: schema, `llms.txt`, fast, server-rendered, crawler-friendly throughout |

### Evidence-Rich Extraction

| Score | You are here if... |
|---|---|
| 1 | Vague, generic content with no specific claims |
| 2 | Some specifics, but claims are unsourced or hedged |
| 3 | Reasonably specific, with some data and direct answers |
| 4 | Most key claims are specific, sourced, and stated up front |
| 5 | Every important passage is quotable, attributable, and the most credible answer available |

### Demand Capture

| Score | You are here if... |
|---|---|
| 1 | No connection between visibility and conversion |
| 2 | Cited pages exist but rarely lead anywhere |
| 3 | Some cited pages guide readers toward action |
| 4 | Cited pages convert, and you track branded lift from AI mentions |
| 5 | AI visibility is a measured, attributed, optimized source of pipeline |

### Reading your score

Add your five scores for a total out of 25.

- **5–10 — Invisible to Crawlable.** Start with Technical Foundation and Information Architecture. Get accessible and organized before chasing citations.
- **11–16 — Retrievable.** You are in the pool but not winning. Focus on Evidence-Rich Extraction. Make your content the most citable answer.
- **17–21 — Cited.** You are a regular source. Tighten Demand Capture so visibility converts, and broaden Citation Surface.
- **22–25 — Compounding.** The flywheel is turning. Defend and extend your lead; document your authority so it stays citable.

Your lowest-scoring pillar is your highest-leverage next move. The framework is designed so you always know what to work on next.

## How to use this

1. **Score yourself** on the five pillars using the scorecard above. Total out of 25.
2. **Place yourself** on the Maturity Model. Most organizations sit at Level 2 or 3 and do not realize how much citation traffic they are leaving on the table.
3. **Work your lowest pillar.** The framework is sequential by design: fix Technical Foundation before Extraction, because there is no point perfecting passages an engine cannot reach.
4. **Re-score quarterly.** AI search moves fast. The Maturity Model gives you a consistent way to measure progress over time.

CITED is a system, not a one-time audit. The brands that win AI search are the ones that treat citation as an operating discipline, run the flywheel, and compound.
